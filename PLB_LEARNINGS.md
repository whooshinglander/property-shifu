# PLB Learnings — PropertyLimBrothers Analysis
_What makes them Singapore's most successful property YouTube channel (79.6K subs, 5K videos) and how to apply it to property factsheets._

---

## Who They Are

PropertyLimBrothers (PLB) is not just a real estate agency. They describe themselves as **"an in-house creative agency + realty combined."** That's the key insight. They treated content and sales as one operation, not two separate things.

- 79,600 subscribers
- 5,000+ videos
- Top videos: 400K-600K views
- Two sub-channels: main (long-form) + Nuggets On The Go (short-form)
- In-house team: 90 creatives + 69 realtors

## What Makes Their Videos Work

### 1. The Signature Home Tour format
Every listing gets a cinematic home tour video — not a slideshow, not a walkthrough, a *story*. They film it like a lifestyle ad. The property is the hero. The agent is the narrator.

**Why it works:** buyers don't buy square footage. They buy the feeling of living there. PLB sells the feeling first, the specs second.

**Factsheet application:** lead with the feeling — the lifestyle headline — before the numbers. "Resort-style living in D18" before "3-bedroom, 1,227sqft, $2,088,000."

---

### 2. Titles that are search + purchase intent combined
Every video title follows the same format:

```
[Development Name] - [Bedroom Type] with [sqft] in [District] | $[Price] | [Agent Name]
```

Example: *"Ecopolitan - 3-Bedroom + Study with 1,216sqft in District 19 | $1,700,000 | Zoe Lim"*

This is not accidental. It hits:
- Property name (branded search)
- Bedroom count (buyer filter)
- Size (buyer filter)
- District (location search)
- Price (affordability filter)
- Agent name (accountability, personal brand)

**Factsheet application:** your factsheet headline = your video title. Every field the buyer uses to filter should be in the headline.

---

### 3. Data + story, not data alone
Their educational content (the 1-hour deep dives) combines hard URA/HDB data with case studies and storytelling. "Why some condo buyers WIN in 2026 (and others OVERPAY BADLY)" — the data gives credibility, the stakes keep you watching.

**Why it works:** data without narrative is a spreadsheet. Narrative without data is an opinion. PLB always combines both.

**Factsheet application:** every key number needs one line of context. Don't just say "psf: $1,650." Say "$1,650 psf — 8% below district average."

---

### 4. Agent personal branding on every listing
Every video features the agent by name. Not just "PLB." Specifically "Zoe Lim," "Yanyan," "George Peng & Jun Wei." The agent becomes associated with their listings' performance.

**Why it works:** buyers and sellers don't hire agencies, they hire people. Personal brand = trust. Trust = higher conversion.

**Factsheet application:** include the agent's name, photo, and one-line track record on every factsheet. Not a corporate logo. A person.

---

### 5. Volume + consistency beats perfection
5,000 videos. Not 50 polished masterpieces. 5,000 consistent, well-structured videos. The format is repeatable. The quality is high but not artisanal. They built a production system, not a production house.

**Why it works:** YouTube rewards consistency. Google rewards freshness. Buyers encounter the brand repeatedly across many searches.

**Factsheet application:** use a template. A factsheet that takes 3 hours to make won't get made. One that takes 20 minutes will be made for every listing.

---

### 6. Educational content builds the audience, listing content converts it
The long-form educational videos ("Should you upgrade from HDB to condo?", "How to own 2 properties without ABSD") build trust and audience. The home tour videos convert that audience into leads.

**Two-funnel model:**
- Top of funnel: educational content → buyers who don't know PLB yet learn to trust them
- Bottom of funnel: listing videos → warm audience sees the property

**Factsheet application:** include one "market context" section in every factsheet — not just the property, but why NOW, why THIS DISTRICT. Educates and sells simultaneously.

---

### 7. The "Disparity Effect" — data as a moat
PLB developed their own proprietary research framework called the Disparity Effect — their analysis of price disparities between comparable properties. It gives them a repeatable analytical edge to justify pricing and investment thesis.

**Why it works:** proprietary data = unique insight = can't be Googled = reason to talk to PLB specifically.

**Factsheet application:** include one data point that the buyer can't easily find themselves. Historical transacted prices, district average psf, price gap vs comparable properties. Make them feel they're getting insider information.

---

## What NOT to Copy

- **Production budget:** PLB has 90 in-house creatives. Your cousin doesn't. Focus on the structure and data, not the cinematic production.
- **Volume for volume's sake:** 5,000 videos works because they have 69 agents. For one agent, quality over quantity.
- **The saga:** PLB had a public controversy in early 2026 about working culture. Don't copy the grind culture, copy the content systems.

## Implementation: The Property Factsheet Template

Based on PLB's formula, every factsheet should have these sections in this order:

### Section 1 — The Headline (feeling first)
```
[Lifestyle hook]
[Development] · [District] · [Tenure] · [$Price]
```
Example:
```
Resort-style living with unblocked greenery views
Grandeur 8 · District 20 · 99-year Leasehold · $2,088,000
```

### Section 2 — The Fast Facts (buyer filters)
```
Bedrooms:     3 + Study
Size:         1,227 sqft
PSF:          $1,701 (8% below D20 average of $1,850)
Floor:        High (12th)
Facing:       North-East (unblocked)
Tenure:       99-year leasehold (2000)
TOP:          2003
Maintenance:  $350/month
```

### Section 3 — The Investment Case (data + narrative)
```
Why this property:
- Last 5 transactions in this stack: $1,620–$1,780 psf. This unit priced below range.
- D20 median psf up 6.2% YoY (URA Q4 2025). Growth district.
- MRT: Bishan (Circle/North-South) — 8 min walk. Rental demand strong.
- Estimated rental: $4,200–$4,500/month → 2.4–2.6% gross yield

Comparable sold units (last 6 months):
- Unit #08-12: $2,050,000 ($1,671 psf) — Mar 2026
- Unit #11-14: $2,150,000 ($1,752 psf) — Jan 2026
- Unit #06-08: $1,990,000 ($1,622 psf) — Nov 2025
```

### Section 4 — The Lifestyle (story)
```
2-3 sentences. Paint the picture. Who lives here, how they live.
"Ideal for young families upgrading from HDB — walking distance to Raffles Institution 
and three primary schools within 1km. The study converts easily to a children's room 
as the family grows."
```

### Section 5 — The Agent (trust)
```
[Photo]  [Name]
         CEA: R012345A
         123 transactions closed | D19-20 specialist
         +65 9123 4567
```

## How the HAT Powers the Factsheet

The HAT adds the data layer that makes Section 3 credible and unique:

- **PSF vs district average** — HAT pulls URA data, computes gap automatically
- **YoY district price trend** — HAT already tracks this from URA REALIS
- **Comparable transactions** — HAT can query recent transactions for the same development
- **Market context** — HAT knows if we're in a rising or softening market and adds one line of context

The agent fills in Sections 1, 2, 4, 5. The HAT auto-populates Section 3.

That's the full system: PLB's presentation formula + HAT's data engine = a factsheet that looks like it took hours but takes 20 minutes.
