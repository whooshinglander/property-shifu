## Singapore Property Cycle — Key Historical Episodes

| Period | What happened | Lesson |
|---|---|---|
| 1996–1998 | Asian Financial Crisis. Prices fell 40–45% peak to trough. Took 10 years to recover. | External shocks + overleveraged buyers = long recovery |
| 2003 | SARS. Sharp but short. V-shaped recovery within 18 months. | Health shocks are temporary if fundamentals are sound |
| 2007–2009 | GFC. Prices fell ~25%. Recovered faster than 1998 due to government stimulus. | Government intervention shortens cycles |
| 2011–2013 | Post-GFC boom. ABSD introduced Dec 2011 (SC 7%, foreigner 15%). Volume fell, prices held. | ABSD slows volume before prices |
| 2018 | ABSD hike (SC 12%→15%, foreigner 15%→20%). Volume -30%, prices flat for 18 months. | Hikes cool sentiment immediately, prices lag |
| 2020 | COVID. Prices barely dipped — government support + low rates + limited supply. | Low rates + supply constraint = resilient prices |
| 2022–2023 | ABSD hike (foreigner to 60%). Foreign demand collapsed. Local demand absorbed. | Foreigners are price-sensitive. Locals are not. |

## Cooling Measure Cycle (Singapore-specific)

Government pattern — reliable over 30 years:
1. Prices rise >8% YoY for 2+ quarters → ABSD hike or LTV tightening
2. Volume falls 20–30% within 2 quarters of measure
3. Prices lag volume — typically flat or -5% over 12–18 months
4. Government holds measures until volume recovers
5. When volume recovers + prices stabilise → gradual relaxation

**Current status (Q1 2026):** ABSD at 60% for foreigners, 20% for SC second property. No new measures since 2023. Prices slowing on their own — government unlikely to hike again unless prices re-accelerate above 5% YoY.

## HDB Upgrader Cycle

BTO completions → MOP at 5 years → upgrader wave hits private OCR market.
- 2019–2021 BTO launches completing 2024–2026 → MOP 2029–2031
- BUT many upgraders start looking 1–2 years before MOP
- This is the structural demand driver for OCR private in 2026–2028

## Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|

| RCR | Rest of Central Region |
| OCR | Outside Central Region (heartlands — most upgrader activity) |
| PSF | Price per square foot |

| ABSD | Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty |

| LTV | Loan-to-Value ratio |

| SORA | Singapore Overnight Rate Average (benchmark for home loans since 2023) |
| CEA | Council for Estate Agencies (property agent licensing body) |
| TOP | Temporary Occupation Permit (completion of new development) |

## 2026 Talking Points — What Every Agent Should Know

These are the key narratives for 2026. Shifu uses these in client conversations. Updated from ERA Research, URA data, and minister statements.

---

### 1. Singapore as a safe haven — global uncertainty drives capital here
*Source: ERA Research, March 2026*

Global markets rattled by US tariffs, Middle East tensions, trade war escalation. Risk-averse investors looking for stable destinations. Singapore's political stability, rule of law, and economic management make it a consistent beneficiary when global uncertainty rises. ERA published data showing Singapore PPI held and recovered through every major crisis since 1990 — Gulf War, Iraq War, COVID, Ukraine.

**Client line:** *"Every time the world got scary, Singapore property dipped briefly then recovered stronger. That's not marketing — that's 30 years of data."*

---

### 2. HDB resale — government cooling it, but demand is structural
*Source: EdgeProp/ERA, Nov 2025*

Government measures: 55,000 BTO launches 2025–2027, reclassification framework, reduced HDB loan quantum, VERS replacing SERS. HDB RPI growth slowed to 1% q-o-q in first 9 months 2025 (vs 2.3% in 2024). Million-dollar HDB flats: 1,336 in Jan–Oct 2025, already past full-year 2024 record of 1,035.

**Client line:** *"HDB prices are still rising — just slower. The government's cooling is working as intended. It's not a crash, it's a managed slowdown."*

---

### 3. Private residential — moderate growth, not a bubble
*Source: URA Q4 2025 flash estimate*

- Full year 2025 PPI: +3.4% — slowest since 2020
- CCR: -3.2% in Q4 2025 (luxury softening)
- OCR: +1.0% in Q4 2025 (heartlands holding)
- New home sales Jan–Oct 2025: exceeded 10,000 — strongest since 2021
- Completions rising: ~5,200 in 2025 → ~7,000 in 2026 (more buyer choice)

**Client line:** *"3.4% for the year — that's not a bubble, that's a market with real demand and limited land."*

---

### 4. Interest rates — the tailwind is coming
*Source: Multiple analysts, Q1 2026*

SORA 3M: ~3.4%, down from peak ~3.8% in 2023. Effective home loan rate ~4.2–4.4%. Market expects further cuts as US economy slows under tariff pressure.

**Client line:** *"Rates have already peaked. Every 0.5% drop in SORA adds roughly 5% to borrowing capacity. Buyers now are getting in before rates make property more competitive."*

**Agent note:** say "market expects" not "rates will drop." Never promise rate movements.

---

### 5. New launches shifting to OCR — upgrader sweet spot
*Source: Stacked Homes, Jan 2026*

65% of 2026 launches in OCR — Tengah (first private condo ever), Tampines, Bayshore, Dairy Farm. Developers kept absolute quantum manageable for upgraders. OCR resale avg PSF 2025: $1,532.

**Client line:** *"2026 is the upgrader's year. Tengah is launching its first private condo. Early movers into new estates historically do well."*

---

### 6. Land cost rising — new launches will get more expensive
*Source: GrowthHQ/ERA Research*

GLS land prices: $1,060 psf ppr (2019) → $1,463 psf ppr (Feb 2026) = +38% in 7 years. Developers pass this through to launch prices.

**Client line:** *"Land costs are up 38% since 2019. That floor gets passed to new launch prices. Waiting doesn't make resale cheaper — it makes new launches more expensive."*

---

### 7. Sub-sales falling — buyers are end-users not speculators
*Source: ERA Research, Feb 2026*

Sub-sales declining. ERA: "Falling sub-sales suggest buying is increasingly driven by owner-occupiers and longer-term investors." Less speculation = less crash risk.

**Client line:** *"People are buying to live or hold, not to flip. That's what a stable market looks like."*

---

### 8. Cooling measures — likely review not tighten
*Source: CNA, Feb 2026; multiple analysts*

CNA headline: "Analysts expect cooling measures to be reviewed as property market shows signs of stabilising." ABSD already at historic highs (60% foreigners, 20% SC 2nd property). Next move more likely relaxation than hike.

**Client line:** *"The next ABSD move is more likely to be a relaxation than a hike. That's a window — once measures ease, demand picks up fast."*

---

### 9. The BTO upgrader wave — 3–5 year structural demand
*Source: Minister Chee Hong Tat; Stacked Homes; ERA*

55,000 BTO launches 2025–2027. 2019–2021 BTO completions hitting MOP now. Each MOP wave releases tens of thousands of upgraders into OCR private. Predictable, structural, recurring demand.

**Client line:** *"Singapore's upgrader cycle is almost clockwork. The 2019–2021 BTO wave is hitting MOP right now. That's real, predictable demand — and it favours OCR private."*

---

### 10. Geopolitical resilience is Singapore's brand
*Source: ERA Research, March 2026*

Safe-haven capital flows increase during global uncertainty. Singapore's track record: property recovered after every major geopolitical event since 1990. Foreign investors see SG property as stable USD-denominated asset.

**Client line:** *"When global investors get scared, they buy Singapore. That's a 30-year pattern. The current tariff uncertainty is sending capital here, not away."*
